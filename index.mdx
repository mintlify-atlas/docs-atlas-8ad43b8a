BACLAB is a ready-to-run bacterial whole genome sequencing (WGS) pipeline hosted as a Google Colab notebook. It takes raw FASTQ files and automatically performs read trimming, quality control, and de novo genome assembly — packaging all results into a downloadable ZIP archive.

## How it works

BACLAB runs a three-stage pipeline inside a managed Conda environment:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Trim reads with Trimmomatic">
    Raw FASTQ files are quality-trimmed and adapter-clipped. Paired-end (PE) runs produce paired and unpaired outputs; single-end (SE) runs produce a single trimmed file.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Quality control with FastQC and MultiQC">
    FastQC generates per-sample QC reports. MultiQC aggregates them into a single interactive HTML summary (`multiqc_report.html`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="De novo assembly">
    PE samples are assembled with **Unicycler** (SPAdes backend). SE samples are assembled with **SPAdes** using the `--careful` flag. Final assemblies are saved as `SAMPLE.fasta`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download your results">
    All trimmed reads, QC reports, assemblies, and logs are packaged into a timestamped ZIP and downloaded automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  BACLAB is designed to work within Google Colab's free-tier resource limits. For large datasets or many samples, mount Google Drive as your data source to avoid session storage limits.
</Note>
